Why Anxiety Feels So Physical

Date: 2026-03-05 · Tags: anxiety, stress, body

Key idea

Anxiety is not just “in your head.” It’s your body’s alarm system (nervous system) turning on. That’s why it can feel like shortness of breath, chest tightness, nausea, dizziness, or shaking.

anxiety stress body

What’s happening in the body

  • Adrenaline increases heart rate and muscle tension to prepare you to act.
  • Breathing changes (often faster or shallower), which can create dizziness or tingling.
  • Digestive system slows, which can feel like nausea or stomach discomfort.
  • Attention narrows—your brain scans for threat, even when you’re physically safe.
Important Feeling these symptoms does not automatically mean something is medically wrong. If symptoms are new, severe, or concerning, get medical evaluation. Then treat anxiety as a nervous-system pattern.

What helps (simple sequence)

  • Label it: “My alarm system is on.” (Naming reduces fear.)
  • Exhale longer: inhale 4 seconds, exhale 6–8 seconds for 2 minutes.
  • Grounding: name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
  • Move gently: slow walking 3–5 minutes tells your body “I can handle this.”
  • Return to task: pick one tiny next step to reduce avoidance.

When to seek more support

  • If anxiety is frequent and interferes with sleep, work, school, or relationships.
  • If panic episodes create strong avoidance or fear of leaving home.
  • If you use substances or compulsions to cope.
